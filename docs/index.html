Getting to NZ

There are a 3 or so airlines that fly to New Zealand direct from U.S.A. We have flown on a few of them, and from those experiences, recommend Air New Zealand. AirNZ has nice planes, service, food, and offer a great option those with babies of a thing called the “sky couch”. Air New Zealand is also a member of the Star Alliance so you can use points you have accumulated from other Star Alliance airlines (such as United airlines).

AirNZ flies direct to Auckland NZ from Los Angles, San Francisco, New York, Vancouver, and Houston. From Auckland you can fly to Taupō (a 50 min. flight). The flight from Auckland to Taupō is less than $80 USD so that probably makes the most sense. We encourage you to send us your flights before you book them, sometimes the slightly cheaper option will send you the wrong way around the world and be much worse.

Things to do in Taupō

Please look at This website if you want ideas on things to do here.

Some of our recommendations/favourites are found below, here and here.

Taupō is a small town in the centre of the north island of NZ. We lived in Dunedin, Wellington, and have now landed here. Taupō is known for it’s natural beauty, big ass lake, tourist attractions, and proximity to the mountain. The natural beauty highlights include geothermal pools, the maunga Ruapehū, and swimming spots. Although there are many tourists, it still feels like a small town. Lake Taupō is the biggest lake in NZ, and feels a lot like lake Michigan in terms of its clarity and summer temperature.

Because of the tourist industry there is lots to do in Taupō. We have bungy jumping, sky diving, river jet boat rides, fly fishing, wine tasting, beaches.. the list is endless really. We’ve provided some recommendations below to at least give you a place to start.

our favourite swimming spots

  • Whakaipo bay
  • Whakamoenga Point
    • park at Rangatira Point car park
    • Follow the track west until it ends. You are there.
  • Kinloch beach
    • General store is cute and has good ice cream.
  • Town beach

walks

  • Waipāhīhī Botanical Reserve
    • Really nice place to go for a walk. Great views, great bush.
  • Mount Tauhaura. It’s the maunga you see right next to town.

cycling

  • You can rent bikes from lots of places. “Pack & Pedal” is our local bike shop so I recommend them.
  • Cycle the W2K trail.
    • Bike about 26 km round trip from Whakaipo to Kinloch. Great bush and lake views. See this link for more information.
  • Cycle along the lakefront. A nice cruizey in-town ride. Can still make it over 25 kms if you do the entire thing.

Hot pools/regular pools

  • Wairakei terraces
    • A few minutes out of town. Our favourite.
  • Debretts.
    • Got waterslides ’n stuff.
  • AC Baths
